Chinese Music

Music is an important part of my heritage and my culture. Many pieces of chinese music are played on traditonal instruments such as the GuQin or the Chinese Lute that give their melodies the unique sounds they embody. I have compiled a table of chinese music below along with their respective descriptions.

Chinese Music Library
Plum Blossoms
Hu Qie's 18 Shots
The Han Palace Autumn Moon The modern version of this piece is attributed to Liu TianHua who adapted it in 1929 to be played on the Cantonese urheen, rather than the original instrument of choice, the lute.
Shepherds Flute
Guanglin San Guangling San - Composed by poet Xi Kang (223AD-262AD). It is an iconic piece that has an extensive hisotry behind it.
Ambush From All Sides Ambush from all Sides (十面埋伏) originating from a battle in 202B.C. at Gaixia during the war between the states of Chu and Han. It is a classical piece that is played on the chinese lute, a common instrument during the time period.
Mountain Stream
Wild Geese Descending on the Sandbank Also know as "Pingsha Luo Yan", this piece was written for the Chinese Guqin instrument by a prince of the Ming Dynasty, Zhu Changfang. This popular melody was released in the Qing Dynasty after Zhu published it in his book, Guyin Zhengzong.
Yangchun Snow Yangchun Snow (Yangchun Bai Xue) was composed in the mid 500s B.C. by musician Shi Kuang (师旷) of the Jin State during the era of the Spring and Autumn period. This piece reflects the bright and cheerful transition from the cold and dark winters to the bright and cheerful spring time.
